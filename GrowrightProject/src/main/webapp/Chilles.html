Research Report: Chillies

Chillies

Chilies come in various shapes and colors, typically long and slender. They range from mild to extremely hot due to capsaicin and add heat and flavor to dishes worldwide. Chilies can be used fresh, cooked, dried, or pickled.

Ideal Growing Conditions

Chilies thrive in warm temperatures (21°C to 29°C during the day, above 10°C at night) and need at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily. They prefer well-draining soil rich in organic matter with a pH of 6.0 to 7.0. Water regularly but allow the soil to dry slightly between watering. Use balanced or vegetable-specific fertilizer regularly, and space plants 45 to 60 cm apart for good air circulation. Support with stakes or cages if needed and watch for pests and diseases.

Growing Chillies at Home

Here’s a step-by-step guide to growing chillies at home:

  • Select Seeds/Seedlings: Choose based on taste and conditions.
  • Prepare Containers/Beds: Use pots with drainage or well-prepared garden beds.
  • Planting: Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before frost ends or transplant seedlings after frost.
  • Placement: Ensure 6-8 hours of sunlight.
  • Watering: Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
  • Fertilization: Apply every 2-3 weeks.
  • Support & Pruning: Stake if needed, pinch early flowers.
  • Pests & Diseases: Monitor and manage pests and diseases.
  • Harvesting: Harvest 60-90 days after planting when peppers are ripe.
